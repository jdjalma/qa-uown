## Summary

The original production defect (account 545697, Andrew Klinger) had the schedule drift years into the future after repeated Bi-Weekly/Semi-Monthly toggling on the customer self-service page, eventually landing on an unsupported Monthly frequency. The fix validates first/second payment day gaps and recalculates the schedule correctly on every change.

Across this validation, the fix held up through 3 consecutive full toggle cycles (6 changes) on a single account, with the schedule always converging on exactly one active installment set and the correct frequency, on both desktop and Android. Both toggle directions (Bi-Weekly to Semi-Monthly and Semi-Monthly to Bi-Weekly) work correctly, the change is always recorded in the account history with the acting party and the before/after frequency, and reloading the page always shows the latest saved frequency.

## Validated scenarios

### Task requirements

#### 1. Frequency dropdown never offers the current value or Monthly

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

With the account on Bi-Weekly, the dropdown offered exactly Weekly and Semi-Monthly. Monthly never appeared, on either viewport.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17371 (desktop) / accountPk=17374 (Android)

#### 2. Semi-Monthly day pickers stay within bounds

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

The First Payment Day picker offered exactly days 1 through 17. With First Payment Day = 1, the Second Payment Day picker offered exactly days 15 through 21 (a 14 to 20 day gap).

**Evidence:** accountPk=17371 / accountPk=17374

#### 3. Bi-Weekly next-payday field

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

Selecting Bi-Weekly as the new frequency revealed a "When is your next payday?" field, and saving sent the account identifier, the new frequency, and the selected payday, without any first/second payment day fields.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17371 / accountPk=17374, payday 2026-07-06

#### 4. Save updates the UI, audit trail, and schedule immediately

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

Saving a valid change showed a success toast and updated the "Current Frequency" label without a page reload. The change was recorded in the account's frequency-change history (actor: customer portal) and in the account activity log with the exact before/after frequency text. The payment schedule was regenerated with exactly one active early-payoff record and no duplicate active installment sets.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17371 / accountPk=17374; frequency-change history rows 148-153 (desktop, account 17372) confirmed directly against the database

#### 5. Frequency persists after reload

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

After saving and navigating to Account Summary and back, the Current Frequency label still showed the newly saved value.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17371 / accountPk=17374

### Additional scenarios explored

#### 6. Three full repeated toggle cycles (the direct reproduction of the original production defect)

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

An account was toggled Bi-Weekly to Semi-Monthly and back, three times in a row (6 total changes), the same pattern that caused the original production incident. All 6 changes completed and were correctly reflected on screen after each save, with no schedule drift and no duplicate active installment sets at any point in the sequence.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17372 (desktop): 6 frequency-change records, 6 matching activity-log entries, exactly 1 active early-payoff record and 28 active regular-payment installments after the 6th save. accountPk=17375 (Android): identical pattern, same counts.

#### 7. Four mixed changes in sequence

> ✅ **PASSED** · desktop and Android

A sequence of 4 alternating changes (Bi-Weekly to Semi-Monthly, back, Bi-Weekly to Semi-Monthly with different days, back) completed cleanly, with the schedule reflecting only the last selected frequency and no leftover duplicate active schedule.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17373 (desktop) / accountPk=17376 (Android)

## Observations (do not block release)

### OBS-1: Second Payment Day option list truncates at day 31 for First Payment Day = 17

> Selecting a First Payment Day of 17 for a Semi-Monthly schedule offers only "31" as the Second Payment Day option, instead of the expected 14 to 20 day gap range, and the Save button stays enabled with that value selected.

<details>
<summary><b>Root cause, reproduction, and proposed fix</b></summary>

**Root cause:** the Second Payment Day option list appears to compute candidate days by simple addition without clamping to the actual number of days in a calendar month, producing an out-of-range day (31 does not exist in April, June, September, or November, and most Februaries).

**When it triggers:** Semi-Monthly frequency with First Payment Day = 17. Not yet re-verified for First Payment Day = 14, 15, or 16 in this validation cycle (remains a hypothesis for those specific values).

**Impact:** a customer selecting First Payment Day = 17 has no valid Second Payment Day option and could save an impossible date. Low customer-facing severity today (First = 17 is an edge value), but worth a UI fix.

**Evidence:** accountPk=17371 (desktop) and accountPk=17374 (Android), both freshly provisioned in this validation cycle. Reproduced 6 times across the full validation history (original discovery plus 3 validation cycles).

**Reproduction:**
```
1. Navigate to Payment Flexibility > Change your payment schedule.
2. Select Semi-Monthly as the new frequency.
3. Select First Payment Day = 17.
4. Open the Second Payment Day dropdown.
Observed: only option "31" is offered.
```

</details>

**Classification:** [CONFIRMED] for First Payment Day = 17 specifically. UI Improvement, low customer impact.
